# Avatar Framework

How to identify the dream client. The avatar is the single most important decision in the entire video — wrong avatar means wrong everything else.

## The Specificity Test

If your avatar description applies to more than 10% of the population, it is too vague.

**Too vague**: "Small business owners"
**Too vague**: "Fitness gym owners"
**Specific enough**: "Fitness gym owners with 50-200 members losing $5K/month to no-shows"
**Specific enough**: "Solo bookkeepers serving 10-30 clients who are turning down work because they can't scale"

The specific avatar feels narrow but actually performs better. A message that resonates with 1 in 100 people who EXACTLY match outperforms a message that's tepid for 100 in 100.

## Four Filters for the Dream Client

When narrowing down, evaluate against these four. The dream client scores high on all four.

### 1. Acute Pain
The pain is current, urgent, and felt daily. Not "would be nice to fix" but "this is keeping me up at night".

### 2. Money to Spend
They have the budget right now. You are not asking them to find money they don't have. The price feels meaningful but not impossible.

### 3. Easy to Reach
You can identify where they hang out, what they read, what they search for. If you can't find them in clusters (specific subreddits, communities, conferences), they are too diffuse.

### 4. Will Talk
They are willing to share their experience publicly. Quiet customers are good customers but bad case studies. Dream clients become testimonials.

## The Avatar Worksheet

Fill these in. Vague answers mean you don't know your avatar yet.

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| Role and context | Independent fitness coach |
| Size or scale | Coaches 30-80 clients, 1-3 years in |
| Daily situation | Manages workout plans, tracks progress, sends check-ins manually |
| Current pain (what's broken) | Spends 15 hours/week on admin instead of coaching |
| Underlying pain (what they feel) | "I became a coach to help people, not be a spreadsheet operator" |
| Dream outcome | Coach 100 clients without losing weekends |
| Failed attempts | Tried Notion, Google Sheets, three other apps; quit each |
| What they say to friends | "I'd hire someone but I can't afford it" |
| What they secretly believe | "Maybe I'm not built for this" |
| Where they hang out | r/personaltrainer, NSCA forums, IG #fitcoach |
| Words they use | "client retention", "programming", "check-ins", "rapport" |

The last two fields are gold for the script: use the avatar's actual vocabulary verbatim.

## Anti-Pattern: Building for Yourself

The worst avatar is "people like me". You are not your customer. Your assumptions about what they want are usually wrong because you have context they don't.

If you find yourself describing the avatar in terms of features they "should" love, stop. Go back to pain and dream outcome — what's true regardless of your product.

## Output

After this exercise, you should be able to picture one specific person and describe their average Tuesday morning. If you can't, you don't have an avatar yet.
