# Unique Mechanism

The mechanism is the named "secret sauce" that makes the product different. Without one, you're "another option in a category." With one, you're "the only product that does X."

## What a Mechanism Is

A mechanism is:
- A named system, process, or method (3-5 words)
- Memorable (alliteration, acronym, or vivid metaphor)
- Specific enough to belong to ONE product
- Explainable in one sentence by a stranger after hearing it once

A mechanism is NOT:
- A feature ("real-time sync")
- A description ("we use AI to do X")
- A category ("project management software")
- A tagline ("get more done")

## Examples (Generic)

| Mechanism Name | One-Line Definition |
|---|---|
| The 3-Step Conversion Stack | A landing page framework: hook above the fold, proof in the middle, offer at the bottom. |
| The PROFIT Method | A 6-letter acronym for the steps a small business takes to get profitable in 90 days. |
| Reverse Engineering Funnels | Starting from the buy button and working backward to figure out what each prior step needs to do. |
| The Quiet Hire | A hiring system that finds candidates who aren't applying anywhere else. |
| Single-Threaded Sprints | A team workflow where only one person works on one feature at a time, end to end. |

Each one is named, memorable, and specific. None of them are generic descriptions of what the product does.

## Why Mechanisms Work

When the avatar hears a generic feature ("we have automation"), their brain compares it to all the other products with automation. You're a commodity.

When the avatar hears a NAMED mechanism ("the Single-Threaded Sprint method"), there's nothing to compare it to. You're the only one. The brain has no reference, so it has to engage with you on your terms.

This is why categories are crowded but mechanisms are blue ocean.

## How To Find Yours

Ask the user these five questions. The answers point to the mechanism.

### 1. What do you do that competitors DON'T do?
List concrete differences. Then group them: which 1-2 are most central?

### 2. What do you do BACKWARDS from how everyone else does it?
The contrarian process is often the mechanism. ("Everyone starts with X. We start with Z.")

### 3. What's the one decision that defines your entire approach?
The foundational choice. ("We decided early on that we'd never X. Everything else flows from that.")

### 4. If a customer described you to a friend, what would they say?
Their words matter. The mechanism often hides in customer language. ("They're the ones who do the [thing] before the [other thing]. Nobody else works like that.")

### 5. What's the one principle you'd defend to the death?
Principles → mechanisms. ("We believe X should always come before Y. So we built a system that enforces that.")

## How To Name It

Once you know what the mechanism IS, you have to name it. Naming rules:

1. **3-5 words max**. Longer = forgettable.
2. **Concrete > abstract**. "Reverse-Engineered Funnels" > "Strategic Optimization Method".
3. **Verb or noun phrase**. Avoid adjectives.
4. **Easy to say out loud**. Test it.
5. **Search-friendly**. If someone Googles it after seeing the video, do they find you?

Naming patterns that work:
- The [Number]-Step [Outcome] Method ("The 4-Step Hiring Method")
- The [Adjective] [Noun] System ("The Quiet Launch System")
- [Verb] + [Object] ("Reverse-Engineering Funnels")
- The [Acronym] Method ("The PROFIT Method")
- The [Metaphor] Approach ("The Sniper Approach")

Avoid: anything with "AI", "smart", "next-gen", "revolutionary", "intelligent", "powered". These words are spam noise.

## The One-Sentence Test

After naming the mechanism, write the one-sentence definition. It must:
- Explain what it IS (not what it does)
- Be understandable by a stranger
- Make the listener want to know more

Bad: "The PROFIT Method helps you grow your business faster." (vague, generic)
Good: "The PROFIT Method is a 6-letter framework that takes a stuck small business from breakeven to profitable in 90 days." (specific, named, time-bound)

## How the Mechanism Shows Up in the Script

In Stage 04 (script), the mechanism appears in Part 4 of the VSL structure (right after the Promise). Example beat:

> "The reason this works while everything else has failed is something we call **the [Mechanism Name]**. Here's how it works: [one-sentence definition]. Most [category] tries to do X. We start with Z instead, and that single change is what unlocks [outcome]."

Three sentences. Names it. Defines it. Differentiates from competitors.

## Output for Stage 03

For the hook-mechanism document, write:

1. 2-3 candidate mechanism names (so the user can pick)
2. One-line definition for each
3. The "compared to competitors" line for each (one sentence)
4. The selected one (after the checkpoint)

The selected mechanism becomes load-bearing for the script. Don't rush this.
