# VSL 7-Part Structure

The script structure for any conversion-focused video, regardless of length. Every length adapts this same skeleton.

## The Seven Parts

```
1. HOOK         (3s)        Stop the scroll
2. PROBLEM      (10-30s)    Agitate the pain
3. PROMISE      (10-20s)    Show the dream outcome
4. MECHANISM    (15-60s)    Name your unique system
5. PROOF        (15-90s)    Show it works
6. OFFER        (15-60s)    Stack the value
7. CTA          (5-15s)     Tell them what to do
```

The durations are typical for a 3-minute video. Shorter videos compress; longer videos expand. See `length-aware-rules.md` for length-specific guidance.

---

## Part 1: Hook (3 seconds)

The first 3 seconds. If they don't stop scrolling here, the rest doesn't matter.

**Source**: Use the selected hook from Stage 03's `hook-mechanism.md`. Don't rewrite it. Don't soften it. Use it verbatim.

**Audit check**: Read the hook line out loud. Does it deliver in 3 seconds or less? If no, cut it down.

---

## Part 2: Problem (10-30s)

Agitate the pain. Make the avatar feel seen.

**Goal**: Get the viewer to nod and think "yeah, that's me." Recognition triggers attention.

**Structure**:
- Name the surface pain (Layer 1 from `pain-dream-gap.md`)
- Drill to the deeper pain (Layer 3-5)
- Optionally, mention failed attempts ("you've probably tried X, Y, Z")

**Anti-pattern**: Don't list features the prospect lacks. List EMOTIONS they have. "You're tired of [feeling], frustrated with [feeling], starting to wonder [doubt]."

**Example beat**:
> "If you're managing client invoices in spreadsheets, you already know what's coming. Late payments. Forgotten invoices. That awful Friday afternoon when you realize you billed someone twice. And the worst part isn't the lost money — it's the feeling that you're running an amateur operation when you're trying to be taken seriously."

---

## Part 3: Promise (10-20s)

Pivot to the dream outcome. Specific. Visceral. Believable.

**Source**: Pull the dream outcome from Stage 01's `positioning.md`.

**Structure**:
- State the outcome concretely
- Show what it FEELS like (not just what it looks like)
- Optionally, set the timeframe ("in 30 days", "by next month")

**Anti-pattern**: Don't promise generic success. Promise the specific scene.

**Example beat**:
> "Imagine waking up Monday morning and your dashboard already shows last week paid in full. Every invoice sent on time. Every client charged the right amount. No late-night spreadsheet checking. No follow-up emails. Just clean numbers. That's what the next 30 days look like."

---

## Part 4: Mechanism (15-60s)

Name your unique system. This is the differentiator.

**Source**: Pull the selected mechanism from Stage 03's `hook-mechanism.md` (the named system + one-line definition).

**Structure**:
- Name the mechanism explicitly ("we call it the [Name]")
- Explain what it IS in one sentence
- Contrast with how everyone else does it
- Hint at why this works (without giving the whole game away)

**Anti-pattern**: Don't try to teach the whole mechanism in the video. Tease it. The point is to convey "we have a specific system" not "you can DIY this".

**Example beat**:
> "The reason this works while every other tool has failed you is something we call the **Pre-Bill Lock**. Most invoicing tools wait until the work is done, then chase the client for payment. We do the opposite: we lock the payment in BEFORE the work starts. One small change. Zero late payments. That's the entire system."

---

## Part 5: Proof (15-90s)

Show that the mechanism works for people like the avatar. Specific, named, believable.

**Sources of proof** (use 2-3, not all):
- **Customer testimonial**: 1-2 sentences from a named customer with their result
- **Numbers**: "1,200+ businesses, $4M+ collected on time"
- **Case study**: a specific before/after with one named customer
- **Demo**: show the product doing the thing
- **Authority**: "featured in X" or "the team behind Y"

**Anti-pattern**: Don't list 10 testimonials. Pick 2-3 strong ones. More dilutes.

**Example beat**:
> "Sarah runs a 12-person marketing agency in Portland. Before this, her DSO was 47 days — almost 7 weeks of late invoices. After switching to the Pre-Bill Lock, it dropped to 3 days. Three. Days. Sarah is one of 1,200+ business owners using this right now. Average across all of them: 91% drop in late payments in the first 60 days."

---

## Part 6: Offer (15-60s)

Stack the value. State the price last. Make the offer feel obvious.

**Source**: Pull the bonus stack, guarantee, and urgency from Stage 02's `offer.md`.

**Structure**:
1. Restate the core offer (one sentence)
2. List 3-5 of the strongest bonuses by perceived value (descending order)
3. State the guarantee
4. State the urgency (if any, only with real reasons)
5. State the price LAST, after the value has been built

**Anti-pattern**: Don't lead with the price. Don't apologize for the price. Don't compare to "other tools that cost $X" (defensive framing).

**Example beat**:
> "Here's what you get: the Pre-Bill Lock system itself, full setup done by our team in your account, the Quick-Start playbook so you start collecting on day one, weekly office hours with our founder for 90 days, and a private community of 500+ business owners doing the same thing. If you don't see a 50% drop in your DSO in the first 30 days, we'll work with you 1-on-1 until you do — for free. The whole thing is $497, and we're capping new signups at 50 per month so we can actually help everyone. After this month's 50 are gone, the next slot is in May."

---

## Part 7: CTA (5-15s)

Tell them exactly what to do next. One action. Repeated.

**Structure**:
- One verb ("click", "tap", "go to")
- One destination ("the link below", "the button on the page")
- One reason ("to lock in your spot", "to get started in 60 seconds")

**Anti-pattern**: Don't offer 3 options. Don't say "or you can also..." Don't end on "if you have questions, reach out." Pick ONE.

**Example beat**:
> "Click the button on this page right now to claim one of this month's 50 spots. You'll be set up by tomorrow morning. The button below — go."

---

## How the Parts Flow

The transitions matter as much as the parts.

- Hook → Problem: "You know that feeling..."
- Problem → Promise: "What if instead..."
- Promise → Mechanism: "Here's how it actually works..."
- Mechanism → Proof: "And it's not just theory..."
- Proof → Offer: "So here's what we built..."
- Offer → CTA: "All you have to do is..."

Each transition is 3-5 words. Don't write transitions longer than that — they slow the video.

## Output for Stage 04

The script document has 7 sections (one per part). Each section contains:
- The spoken text for that beat
- Inline `[ON-SCREEN: ...]` markers where on-screen text supports the spoken line
- Approximate duration

Total word count should match the target length: ~150 words/minute is the spoken pace for clear delivery. A 60-second video is ~150 words. A 90-second video is ~225 words. A 3-minute video is ~450 words.
