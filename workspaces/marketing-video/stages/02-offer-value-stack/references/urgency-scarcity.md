# Urgency and Scarcity

Urgency and scarcity convert "I'll think about it" into "I'll act now." Done badly, they erode trust. Done well, they reflect honest constraints and accelerate decisions.

## The Difference

**Urgency** = limited TIME. ("Offer ends Friday at midnight.")
**Scarcity** = limited SUPPLY or ACCESS. ("Only 12 seats in this cohort.")

Either one alone is good. Both layered together is stronger. Both LIED ABOUT is fatal.

## Real vs. Artificial

The single most important rule: **always use real reasons whenever possible**.

### Real urgency examples
- A cohort has a hard start date (real: the next session begins Monday)
- A discount expires because the next price tier kicks in (real: pricing change is scheduled)
- A bonus is removed after a date because the included person is no longer available (real: the consultant who runs the bonus call is unavailable)
- A campaign ends because the marketing budget runs out (real: paid traffic stops on a date)

### Real scarcity examples
- Limited cohort size for quality reasons (real: instructor can only handle 30 students)
- Hand-built/handmade product with capacity limits (real: factory output)
- Limited 1-on-1 calls per month (real: founder availability)
- Beta program with intentional cap (real: feedback loop manageability)

### Fake urgency / scarcity (avoid)
- "Only 3 left" with a fake counter that resets
- "Offer ends today" that resets every day
- Countdown timers tied to no real event
- "Last chance" emails sent every week

Fake urgency works once. Then it kills trust permanently. Don't.

## The "Why Now" Test

For any urgency claim, you should be able to answer "WHY NOW?" in one sentence with a real reason.

| Urgency Claim | Why Now (Real) | Acceptable? |
|---|---|---|
| Price goes up Monday | New tier launches Monday | YES |
| Bonus calls expire Friday | Consultant leaves Friday | YES |
| Only 12 seats | Cohort cap is 12 for instructor bandwidth | YES |
| Limited time offer | (no real reason) | NO |
| Only 3 left | (false counter) | NO |

If you can't answer "Why Now?" honestly, don't add urgency. The script will be stronger without it.

## Layering Real Reasons

The strongest urgency stacks 2-3 real reasons:

> "The early-bird price closes Friday because we're switching to the new pricing tier next week. The bonus 1-on-1 call is only for the first 10 buyers because that's how many our coach can handle in a week. And the bonus session with [guest] is Saturday — anyone who joins after that misses it permanently."

Three real reasons. Each verifiable. Each adds pressure without lying.

## Scarcity Patterns That Work

### Cohort-Based
"Next intake starts April 15. After that, the next one is June 1."

### Capacity-Based
"We onboard 5 new clients per month so we can give each one personal attention."

### Time-Locked Bonuses
"This bonus is included for everyone who joins before the end of the month. After that, it goes back into the regular paid product."

### Seasonal
"This offer is only run twice a year because it includes [seasonal asset]."

## When NOT to Add Urgency

- High-trust brands with long sales cycles (urgency feels desperate)
- Enterprise sales (decision makers are slow, urgency offends)
- First-time buyers in a category they don't understand (urgency breeds skepticism)
- Anything where the prospect needs to "think about it" with their team

In these cases, lean on the offer's value and the guarantee. Urgency is the wrong lever.
