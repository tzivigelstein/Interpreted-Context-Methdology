# Bonus Stacking

Bonuses outperform discounts. A discount cheapens the perception of the core product. A bonus stacks more reasons to buy without lowering the anchor.

## The Core Principle

A "Grand Slam Offer" is one where the perceived value of all the bonuses combined far exceeds the price of the offer itself. Hormozi's rule of thumb: total stack value should be 10x the price.

If the price is $100, the total stack value (including bonuses) should be at least $1,000.

## Why Bonuses Beat Discounts

| Discount | Bonus |
|---|---|
| Lowers the anchor price (next time you sell, you have to start lower) | Keeps the anchor price intact |
| Signals "the original price was inflated" | Signals "the original price was a bargain" |
| Trains customers to wait for sales | Trains customers to act on the stack |
| Removes value | Adds value |
| Costs you margin | Often costs you nothing (digital bonuses) |

## The Bonus Categories

Stack 5-10 bonuses, ideally one from each category that fits.

### 1. Speed Bonuses
Bonuses that get the result FASTER. Push down the Time Delay lever.
- "Quick-start playbook"
- "Day-one wins guide"
- "Pre-built templates ready to go"

### 2. Ease Bonuses
Bonuses that reduce the EFFORT required. Push down the Effort & Sacrifice lever.
- "Done-for-you setup"
- "White-glove migration"
- "We'll write your first one for you"

### 3. Confidence Bonuses
Bonuses that increase the PERCEIVED LIKELIHOOD. Reduce risk and skepticism.
- "Personal onboarding call"
- "Direct access to our team for 30 days"
- "Refund if you don't see X by Y"
- (Note: a guarantee is a separate beat in Stage 02, but bonuses can stack confidence too)

### 4. Variety Bonuses
Bonuses that broaden the OUTCOME. Push up the Dream Outcome lever.
- "Bonus module on adjacent topic"
- "Community access"
- "Office hours every week"
- "Bonus for the OTHER role" (e.g. you're selling to founders but include a bonus for their head of marketing)

## The Bonus Stack Table

For each bonus, document:

| Bonus Name | What It Is | Lever It Pushes | Perceived Value | Real Cost To You |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Quick-Start Playbook | 14-page PDF guide | Speed | $97 | $0 (one-time write) |
| First Project Done-For-You | We build your first deliverable | Ease + Confidence | $499 | 2 hours of staff time |
| Private Community | Slack channel with other users | Variety + Confidence | $197/yr | $0 (already running) |
| Weekly Office Hours | Live Q&A every Wednesday | Confidence + Variety | $497/yr | 1 hour/week |
| Migration From [Competitor] | We transfer your existing data | Ease | $299 | 30 mins of script time |

The "Real Cost To You" column is critical. Each bonus should have HIGH perceived value and LOW real cost. That's the multiplier that makes a Grand Slam Offer profitable.

## The Stack Reveal Order

In the script (Stage 04, "Offer" beat), list bonuses from MOST valuable to LEAST. Each one builds momentum. The smallest bonus listed last keeps the energy moving but lets the prospect feel "OK that's enough now, I'm in."

If any bonus is BORING (no excitement when you say it), cut it. Boring bonuses dilute the stack.

## The Anti-Pattern: Feature Stacking

A bonus is NOT a feature. "Includes dark mode" is a feature. "30 days of personal coaching from our founder" is a bonus.

Test: would you advertise this thing on its own and price it? If yes, it's a bonus. If no, it's a feature and belongs on the product page, not in the offer stack.

## Output for Stage 02

The offer document should include the bonus stack table (5-10 rows) with all four columns filled in. Total perceived value at the bottom. Compare against the price.

If total perceived value < 10x price → either add more bonuses or push more value into existing ones.
