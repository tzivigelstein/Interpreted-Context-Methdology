# The Value Equation

The single most important formula in the entire workspace. Every offer decision should pass through this equation.

## The Formula

```
            Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value  =  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

Four levers. Two go in the numerator (push them UP to increase value). Two go in the denominator (push them DOWN to increase value).

You don't need to push all four. You need to push the ones the avatar feels most strongly about.

## Lever 1: Dream Outcome (push UP)

How big, vivid, and emotionally meaningful is the outcome the offer delivers?

**Weak**: "Save time."
**Strong**: "Reclaim 12 hours a week and use them for what you actually love about your job."

How to push it up:
- Make it specific (numbers, durations, scenes)
- Make it visceral (what they feel, see, hear)
- Tie it to identity ("become the kind of coach who...")
- Tie it to status ("the founder everyone asks for advice")
- Stack outcomes ("not just X, but also Y and Z")

## Lever 2: Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (push UP)

How confident is the avatar that THEY specifically will get the outcome (not "people in general")?

**Weak**: "It works."
**Strong**: "Out of 1,247 coaches who've used this in the last year, 1,180 hit their goal in the first 60 days. We have the data. Here are 5 of them on video."

How to push it up:
- Specific data (numbers, percentages, durations)
- Named case studies with faces
- A guarantee (Stage 02 covers this in `guarantee-types.md`)
- A clear mechanism (Stage 03 covers this in `unique-mechanism.md`)
- Social proof at scale (X people, Y testimonials, Z reviews)
- Authority markers (credentials, press, partnerships)

## Lever 3: Time Delay (push DOWN)

How long until the avatar starts seeing results?

**Weak**: "Results in 6 months."
**Strong**: "First win on day one. Full transformation by week 4."

How to push it down:
- Onboarding speed (signup → first value in minutes)
- Quick-win milestones (early wins that build momentum)
- Front-loaded value (most important results first)
- "Done-for-you" elements that bypass slow steps

## Lever 4: Effort & Sacrifice (push DOWN)

How much work, learning, or trade-off does the avatar have to do themselves?

**Weak**: "You'll need to set aside 5 hours a week to learn the system."
**Strong**: "Plug in your inputs once. We do the rest. No setup, no learning curve, no homework."

How to push it down:
- Done-for-you services
- Pre-built templates / defaults
- Removing decisions (opinionated workflows)
- Automating tedious steps
- "What you give up to get this" (subtract sacrifices, not just add features)

## The Lever Audit

Score the CURRENT offer on each lever 1-10 from the avatar's perspective:

| Lever | Current Score | Target Move |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | | |

| Time Delay (lower is better) | | |
| Effort & Sacrifice (lower is better) | | |

Any lever scoring under 7 is an opportunity. Don't try to fix all four — fix the one or two that move the needle most for THIS avatar.

## The Hormozi Test

The goal: make the avatar feel **stupid saying no**. Not interested. Not curious. STUPID. As in "if I don't take this, I'm a fool."

If the offer doesn't pass that test, one or more of the four levers needs more work. Go back to whichever scored lowest and find a concrete way to push it.
